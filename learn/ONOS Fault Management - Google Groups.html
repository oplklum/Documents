Hi Akiro,

the fault management application had to be partially reworked that's why the wiki page is old. we will update it ASAP.

Regarding your query for notifications and Alarms: this transformation is exactly what Helen Wu at On.Lab worked on. Right now you can see the implementation in NetconfAlarmTranslator e NetconfAlarmProvider.java.
The idea is that you start the subscription of notifications on your own device via the startSubscriptionMethod() of the NetconfSession.java. Once you do that your device will send notifications to ONOs and those will be picked up by the NetconfAlarmProvider.java that will translate them in Alarms and push them to the FaultManagement App. the FaultManagement app has a replicated store that distributes the alarms across the ONOS instances. 
The FM app also offer the possibility to register a listener in order to receive the alarms that are added. For the listener please see TestListener here .
If your device does not comply to the standard Netconf notifications format a behaviour to translate them is in the works but not in the master yet.

I also saw your review at https://gerrit.onosproject.org/#/c/10492/ . Will take a look shortly and give feedback. 

Did this clarify some of your doubts ? Is there anything else I can help you with ?

Cheers,
Andrea Campanella

Hi Akihiro, if by other ONOSs you mean other instances inside the same cluster that is already done via the replicated alarm store (AlarmStore.java) in the FM app. The instance that recieves the alamr from the netconf devices sends it up to the
Hi Akihiro,

if by other ONOSs you mean other instances inside the same cluster that is already done via the replicated alarm store (AlarmStore.java) in the FM app. The instance that recieves the alamr from the netconf devices sends it up to the FM app that replicates it to the other instances in the cluster. there is no need fro the NETCONF device to have additional support.

If instead you meant between ONOS clusters as peers or hierachical controllers that is something that is in the works but still needs to be developed. There are some works done by multiple teams, e-cord team, ACTN team from china and the Icona project from Create-net.

Cheers,
Andrea Campanella

[Adding ONOS developers]

There is a profound difference between AlarmConsumer.java and AlarmTranslator.java

AlarmConsumer.java is user by PollingAlarmProvider.java and is intended for those device that do no support pushing of notifications to a listener. If you look at the LumentumAlarmConsumer.java you will see that the class makes calls to the device to discover which alarms are present on the device. The call happens periodically though a scheduler in the PollingAlarmProvider.java

AlarmTranslator.java is instead intended to take an inputstream, let's say an XML tree, and translate it into an ONOS Alarm.java instance. This has nothing to do with behaviours and device, if you take a look at the NetconfAlarmTranslator.java here you will see the translation of the standard NETCONF notification XML tree into the alarm.

We are currently working on having a behaviour that you will plug into the protocol implementation of the AlarmTransaltor that will superseed the protocol general one in favor of a per-device specific one. I can't give you a time frame right now, if you are willing to help us on this matter it would be much appreciated. 

Cheers,
Andrea Campanella

On Fri, Jul 15, 2016 at 8:18 AM, Andrea Campanella <and...@onlab.us> wrote:
Hi Akiro,

the fault management application had to be partially reworked that's why the wiki page is old. we will update it ASAP.

Regarding your query for notifications and Alarms: this transformation is exactly what Helen Wu at On.Lab worked on. Right now you can see the implementation in NetconfAlarmTranslator e NetconfAlarmProvider.java.
The idea is that you start the subscription of notifications on your own device via the startSubscriptionMethod() of the NetconfSession.java. Once you do that your device will send notifications to ONOs and those will be picked up by the NetconfAlarmProvider.java that will translate them in Alarms and push them to the FaultManagement App. the FaultManagement app has a replicated store that distributes the alarms across the ONOS instances. 
The FM app also offer the possibility to register a listener in order to receive the alarms that are added. For the listener please see TestListener here .
If your device does not comply to the standard Netconf notifications format a behaviour to translate them is in the works but not in the master yet.

I also saw your review at https://gerrit.onosproject.org/#/c/10492/ . Will take a look shortly and give feedback. 

Did this clarify some of your doubts ? Is there anything else I can help you with ?

Cheers,
Andrea Campanella
